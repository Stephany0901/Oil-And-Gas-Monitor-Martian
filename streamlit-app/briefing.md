# Oil & Gas Briefing — July 22, 2026

## Market

Crude firmed sharply into July 22. Brent settled around **$91.10/bbl** and WTI near **$84.29/bbl** on July 21, both up roughly **2%** on the day, driven almost entirely by a Middle East supply-risk premium rather than fundamentals. Markets are tracking continued US–Iran hostilities, a reported tanker strike near the Strait of Hormuz, Houthi threats against Red Sea traffic, and disruption to Caspian Pipeline Consortium exports out of Kazakhstan. Offsetting the geopolitical bid, reports of Iranian mediation talks and a possible 10-day ceasefire are capping the upside.

On supply, OPEC+ has continued its gradual unwind of the 2023 voluntary cuts, with seven core members (Saudi Arabia, Russia, Iraq, Kuwait, Kazakhstan, Algeria, Oman) approving a further **188 kb/d** monthly increase — the fifth consecutive hike as the group defends market share. That barrels-back-to-market trend is the structural bearish counterweight to the war premium, and the bid unwinds fast if the Hormuz corridor clears.

Natural gas remains the soft side of the complex. Henry Hub has held a tight **$3.15–$3.34/MMBtu** range since mid-June; the EIA's July outlook, recently revised higher, pegs the 2026 average near **$3.67/MMBtu**, implying a recovery into winter.

For equities, the majors have de-rated ~20% off their spring highs as the near-term earnings picture reset to lower realized prices. ExxonMobil and Chevron report Q2 on weaker sequential crude, so guidance and buyback commentary matter more than the headline print.
