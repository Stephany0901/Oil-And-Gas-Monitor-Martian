# Oil & Gas Briefing — July 21, 2026

*Quote feed is fresh this run (Monday, July 20 close/intraday), so the movers below are new — not carried forward.*

## Market

Crude held firm to start the week. WTI traded near $82/bbl and Brent around $88, still carrying a Strait of Hormuz risk premium after this year's disruptions pushed Brent to a $126 peak. The bid rests on lingering Middle East risk, OPEC+ supply discipline and inventory draws, set against a market most analysts still expect to move toward surplus. On July 5, seven OPEC+ members (Saudi Arabia, Russia, Iraq, Kuwait, Kazakhstan, Algeria and Oman; the UAE exited May 1) agreed a modest 188 kb/d August increase — a continued, gradual unwind of voluntary cuts as Hormuz tanker traffic normalizes rather than an aggressive push for share. The practical read is unchanged: this is a geopolitical bid layered on a loose physical balance, and it unwinds fast if the corridor clears.

Natural gas remains the weak side of the complex. Henry Hub sits near a two-month low around $2.85–2.91/MMBtu, pressured by Lower-48 output above 110 Bcf/d, storage roughly 6.6% above the five-year average, cooler near-term weather, and reduced export pull from Freeport LNG maintenance running into late August. EIA nonetheless nudged its 2026 Henry Hub forecast up to about $3.67/MMBtu, implying a recovery into winter.

Equity leadership again favored refiners and integrateds over oilfield services. Refiners (VLO, MPC, PSX) sit at or near 52-week highs on wide crack spreads, while services names (SLB, HAL, BKR) lagged — the signature of a price-led move with soft completions activity and earnings-season caution.

## Screen idea

Of the 23 names checked, **only BKR closed below its 200-day average** (~$55.15 vs ~$56.01) — but at ~11x EV/EBITDA it is the priciest of the cheap-looking cohort, so it fails the valuation half of the test. The genuinely cheap names, **DVN at ~4.8x** and **OXY at ~5.8x** EV/EBITDA, both sit above their 200-day averages and fail the pullback half. No name currently satisfies both conditions — a reminder that after the sector's rally most names trade above their long-term trend. The closest approximation is **HAL** (~8.6x, and roughly 7% below its 50-day though still above its 200-day). More broadly, oilfield services is conspicuously not participating while the E&Ps it serves make highs — worth understanding why before reading that gap as opportunity. *Educational only — not investment advice.*
