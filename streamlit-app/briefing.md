# Oil & Gas Briefing — July 10, 2026

## Market

Crude gave back part of yesterday's spike, pulling most energy equities lower. WTI is trading near $72/bbl and Brent around $77–78 after Brent snapped a two-day rally — even as the US confirmed a second consecutive day of strikes on Iran. The muted reaction reflects uncertainty over whether the escalation will actually disrupt flows through the Strait of Hormuz; traders are pricing the risk premium cautiously rather than chasing prices higher. President Trump warned of possible further action, including targeting Iran's Kharg Island export terminal, which keeps a supply-shock tail risk alive. Natural gas sold off hard (detailed below). OPEC+ continues to hold output discipline while US shale supply keeps growing.

Natural gas told a sharper story: front-month (August) Henry Hub futures dropped ~4.3% to $3.073/MMBtu on July 9 — some feeds put it over 6%, the steepest daily fall since March — closing at a six-week low. The selloff was driven by a bigger-than-expected 61 Bcf storage build (week ended July 3, vs ~49 Bcf consensus) and Freeport LNG's planned major maintenance starting July 10 through late August, which trims feedgas/export demand. Inventories remain above the five-year average, and record Permian-led production keeps a lid on prices.

On the tape, upstream names and majors fell ~2.5–3% as crude eased, while refiners bucked the trend — lower feedstock costs widen crack spreads. ExxonMobil pre-announced a ~$7B Q2 profit tailwind (reports July 31); Occidental drew an Evercore upgrade (target to $65) and is taking a 10% stake in Exxon's offshore Trinidad exploration block.
