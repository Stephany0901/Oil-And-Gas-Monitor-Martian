# Oil & Gas Briefing — July 8, 2026

## Market

Crude firmed off multi-month lows: Brent rose toward $73 (one-week high) and WTI traded in the $68–69 range. A Qatar-owned LNG carrier was struck by a projectile near the Strait of Hormuz, reviving doubts about the US-Iran agreement's durability — even as at least eight Japan-linked vessels, including five supertankers, transited the strait, signaling recovering flows. Supply pressure remains the dominant theme: OPEC+ approved a fifth consecutive monthly hike (+188k bpd for August across seven members, ~800k bpd cumulative since April), though June output is still ~28% below February levels. Saudi Aramco cut Arab Light pricing for Asia by $11/bbl to a $1.50 discount, underscoring soft conditions. US natural gas sits near $3.22–3.25/MMBtu — record-adjacent Lower 48 production (~110 Bcf/d) and an above-average storage build (+87 Bcf) offset heat-driven power burn, with above-normal temperatures forecast through mid-July.

## Today's movers

Energy equities rallied broadly (XLE +2.8%, XOP +2.2%, XES +2.0%). Biggest gainers: OXY +5.9%, DVN +5.1%, COP +4.7%. Decliners were confined to refiners: VLO −1.5%, MPC −1.0% (profit-taking near 52-week highs); SLB +1.5% was the softest of the rest.

## Stocks to watch

- **OXY** — led the sector's bounce (+5.9%); high crude beta cuts both ways.
- **DVN** — +5.1% and cheapest of the group at ~4.7x EV/EBITDA.
- **LNG** — Cheniere +3.6%; Hormuz LNG-tanker incident keeps global gas security in focus.
- **VLO / MPC** — refiners bucked the rally near record highs; crack-spread watch.
- **BKR** — only major still below its 200-day average despite the sector move.
