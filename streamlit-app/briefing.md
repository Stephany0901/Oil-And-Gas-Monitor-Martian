# Oil & Gas Briefing — July 9, 2026

## Market

Energy is trading risk-on as a Middle East supply shock takes over. WTI jumped ~5–6% over the past 24 hours to around $74/bbl and Brent to roughly $72–74, after the US launched strikes tied to Iran and revoked the waiver that allowed Iranian crude exports — following attacks on vessels in the Strait of Hormuz, including a Qatari LNG carrier and a Saudi tanker. The moves put the interim US–Iran framework at risk and reintroduce a genuine supply-disruption premium after crude spent recent weeks near multi-month lows. Traders are braced for volatility around API inventories, FOMC minutes, the IEA monthly report and OPEC+ signaling. Natural gas is comparatively quiet near $3.22/MMBtu (+0.2% on the day): supply growth keeps outpacing demand, though LNG feedgas flows rose to ~18.1 Bcf/d in July on stronger export pull, while cooler forecasts through mid-July cap cooling demand. Lower-48 production eased to ~109.4 Bcf/d.

Refiners are the standout. Crude's spike widened crack-spread expectations, sending VLO, MPC and PSX up ~5–6%. On the corporate side, ExxonMobil's July 7 8-K flagged Middle East unrest as a Q2 driver, with the Street modeling a ~$15.9B adjusted-profit windfall; J.P. Morgan placed a Buy on Valero and Evercore upgraded Occidental.
